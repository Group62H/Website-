Introduction of Streaming

What is Streaming?

The act, process, or occurrence of streaming data or of accessing data that is being streamed. The phrase refers to the continuous transfer of audio and video files from a server to a client. In video streams, information is transmitted over the internet in a compressed form and is instantly viewed by the viewer. A constant stream of data is used to send the material, which is played as it comes in. Both live performance and recording are options.

What are the pros and cons of Live Streaming?

Pros

  • Viewers can easily access live streams because there are numerous free platforms available on the internet.
  • Streams offer real-time engagement and entertainment for the viewers, and
  • You can watch and listen to a streaming content anywhere and anytime.

Cons

  • Technical errors usually happen and can affect your engagement, especially if you do not reach the critical requirements.
  • Can only be accessed if you have a WiFi connection and/or have mobile data that is required by the platform.
  • In some cases, it can be addictive, when you’re finding yourself unable to stop watching, you can lose track of time and, worst of all, can affect your holistic health.

What makes a good stream?

In actuality, there are several characteristics of a good stream depending on the type of content you're concentrating on, but the most important qualities of a good stream for all content are:

  • Good Audio Quality: It is responsible for the audibility of the content being played by the viewer.
  • Good Video Quality: It is responsible for the quality of the video of the content being played by the viewer.
  • Accessibility: Being able to access helpful streams without any catch or contract made.
  • Mobile Compatibility: Not everyone can afford to buy a PC or a Laptop so they use a phone to do their work, play games, and, in this case, watch streams.
